

## **Case Study: UPI Fraud**

### **Background**

Unified Payments Interface (UPI) has become one of the most popular digital payment methods in India due to its convenience and speed. However, the rise in its usage has also led to an increase in fraud cases.

---

### **Case Description**

A college student, Rahul, wanted to sell his used laptop online. He listed the product on a marketplace and was contacted by a buyer who showed interest.

The “buyer” agreed to pay via UPI and sent Rahul a QR code, asking him to scan it to “receive” the payment. Trusting the buyer, Rahul scanned the QR code and entered his UPI PIN.

Instead of receiving money, ₹15,000 was deducted from his bank account. The buyer immediately became unreachable.

---

### **Method Used by Fraudster**

* The fraudster used a **fake payment request** instead of sending money.
* Sent a **QR code for payment collection**, not transfer.
* Exploited lack of awareness that:

  * Scanning a QR code can also mean **paying money**
  * Entering a UPI PIN always authorizes a debit

---

### **Impact**

* Financial loss of ₹15,000
* Emotional stress and loss of trust in digital payments
* Time spent reporting the fraud and filing complaints

---

### **Preventive Measures**

* Never scan QR codes to receive money
* Do not share UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details with anyone
* Verify payment notifications in the official app
* Use trusted platforms for transactions
* Be cautious of deals that seem too good to be true

---

### **Action Taken**

Rahul reported the fraud to:

* His bank
* Cybercrime helpline (1930)
* National Cyber Crime Portal

Although recovery was uncertain, early reporting increased the chances of blocking the fraudulent transaction.

---

### **Conclusion**

UPI frauds often rely on **social engineering and lack of awareness** rather than technical hacking. Users must stay informed and cautious while performing digital transactions to avoid such scams.
